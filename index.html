Selected Work

My Projects

Housing Price Prediction (Machine Learning, EDA – Python) – UCL

Built an end-to-end supervised regression pipeline in Python to predict residential property prices from property characteristics, location, and amenity features.

  • Performed EDA and feature engineering (property_age, land_floor_ratio), handled missing data and outliers using median imputation and percentile capping.
  • Trained and compared four models (Linear Regression, Decision Tree, Random Forest, XGBoost) with 5-fold cross-validation and hyperparameter tuning via RandomizedSearchCV.
  • Evaluated model performance on a held-out test set to ensure generalisability and confirm no overfitting.
Python Scikit-learn XGBoost Pandas Machine Learning

Sustainability & Cost Analytics (Statistics, R, Excel) – UCL

Evaluated recycling performance and sustainability initiatives for European firms across construction, manufacturing, and transportation sectors using R and Excel.

  • Conducted EDA with correlation analysis, contingency tables, z-score classification, and hypothesis testing (t-tests, proportion tests) to assess initiative impact on OPEX.
  • Built and diagnosed a log–log OPEX regression model to examine the relationship between sustainability investment and operating expenditure, assessing whether initiative participation had standalone cost impact.
R Excel Regression Statistical Testing

Targeted Marketing Campaign Optimisation (K-Means, RFM – R) – UCL

Optimised a targeted marketing campaign for an e-commerce platform using customer segmentation to maximise ROI compared to blanket marketing.

  • Computed break-even response rates and demonstrated blanket marketing yields negative ROI (–21%), motivating a data-driven segmentation approach.
  • Engineered RFM features from transactional data, applied K-means clustering (with elbow method for optimal k) to segment customers into behavioural groups, and identified the highest-response segment for targeting.
  • Compared ROI across blanket, RFM quintile, K-means, Decision Tree, and Random Forest targeting strategies to recommend the most profitable campaign approach.
R K-Means Clustering RFM Analysis Marketing Analytics

Competitive Market Share Prediction (Logistic Regression – Python) – UCL

Predicted quarterly market share gains and losses for a major pharmaceutical company in the GLP-1 drug market using logistic regression with Lasso regularisation.

  • Engineered lagged and rolling-average features from financial and sales data, applying StandardScaler preprocessing for model training.
  • Built and compared multiple Lasso logistic regression models across internal, competitive, and direct feature sets, evaluating performance with classification reports and ROC-AUC scores.
  • Conducted what-if scenario analysis to simulate the impact of margin improvements and new market entrants on predicted market share probability.
Python Scikit-learn Logistic Regression Feature Engineering Scenario Analysis Scenario Simulation

False Alarm Prediction – London Fire Brigade (Decision Tree, Random Forest – Python) – UCL

Analysed 236,763 London Fire Brigade incident records to identify temporal, geographic, and property-level drivers of false alarm callouts, and built classification models to predict them.

  • Cleaned and engineered features from raw incident data including datetime decomposition, borough-level aggregation, and property category encoding to prepare a modelling-ready dataset.
  • Conducted exploratory analysis of false alarm rates across hours, days of week, London boroughs, and property types, surfacing actionable patterns in callout behaviour.
  • Trained and evaluated Decision Tree and Random Forest classifiers, identifying the strongest predictors of false alarms and generating data-driven recommendations for resource allocation.
Python Pandas Scikit-learn Decision Tree Random Forest EDA
